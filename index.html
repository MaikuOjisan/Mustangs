History

The Ford Mustang debuted in 1964 and quickly became a symbol of performance and style. Its long hood, short rear deck, and aggressive stance set the standard for pony cars. Over the decades, the Mustang has evolved, but its spirit of freedom and adventure remains unchanged. The original price was $2,368, making it accessible to a wide audience. The Mustang's popularity soared with its appearance in movies like "Bullitt" and "Gone in 60 Seconds," solidifying its status as a cultural icon. Today, the Mustang continues to innovate with modern technology while honoring its classic roots.

Fun Facts

  • The Mustang was named after the P-51 Mustang fighter plane from WWII.
  • Over 10 million Mustangs have been produced since 1964.
  • The Mustang has appeared in over 3,000 movies and TV shows.
  • The 1968 Mustang GT Fastback was made famoppuse by Steve McQueen in the movie "Bullitt".
  • Mustangs are popular for customization and racing worldwide.
  • The Mustang has been in continous production since 1964, unlike some of it's popular rivals.
  • Mustangs have a dedicated fan base with numerous clubs and events globally.
  • The Mustang was the first pony car, inspiring many competitors like the Chevrolet Camaro and Dodge Challenger.
