History

The Ford Mustang debuted in 1964 and quickly became a symbol of performance and style. Its long hood, short rear deck, and aggressive stance set the standard for pony cars. Over the decades, the Mustang has evolved, but its spirit of freedom and adventure remains unchanged.

Fun Facts

  • The Mustang was named after the P-51 Mustang fighter plane from WWII.
  • Over 10 million Mustangs have been produced since 1964.
  • The Mustang has appeared in over 3,000 movies and TV shows.
  • It was the first car to win the Tiffany Gold Medal for excellence in American design.
  • Mustangs are popular for customization and racing worldwide.
